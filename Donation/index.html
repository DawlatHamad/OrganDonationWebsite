Checking Donor Status

Adding your name to the Michigan Organ Donor Registry is very simple. This can be done when you are applying or renewing a driver's license.

Where to look:

  • Check for the red heart symbol at the bottom corner of license
  • If not, fill out the Donor Registry form
  • Request a new license which will now include the red heart symbol

Age

There is no age limit for those that can register as donors. People under the age of 18 can become donors, however a legal guardian can revoke that decision. Be sure to dicuss your plans to become a donor with family regardless of age.

Transplant Process

Step 1
Referral

Donor: A person can choose to become a donor voluntarily or for a specific recipient.

Recipient: A patient with organ failure is referrred to a transplant center.

Step 2
Evaluation

Donor: Undergoes medical and psychological (for living donors) evaluation.

Recipient: Patients medical history and health condition is evaluated to deem patient suitable for transplant.

Step 3
Placement

Donor: Once approved, donor is scheduled for transplant surgery.

Recipient: If deemed eligible, patient is placed on a waiting list based on medical urgency and compatibility.

Step 4
Preparation

Donor: Living donors recieve information about the upcoming surgery and post-operative care.

Recipient: Patient is notified and prepared for surgery.

Step 5
Surgery

Donor: Undergo surgery to remove the donated organ(s).

Recipient: Undergoes surgical procedure to receive organ.

Step 6
Postoperative

Donor: Living Donor is monitered and receives postoperative care, receive follow-up care to ensure revovery.

Recipient: Monitored for signes of organ rejections and complications, receives follow-ups care to moniter adjustments.
