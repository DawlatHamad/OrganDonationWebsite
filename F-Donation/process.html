Transplant Process

Step 1
Referral

Donor: A person can choose to become a donor voluntarily or for a specific recipient.

Recipient: A patient with organ failure is referred to a transplant center.

Step 2
Evaluation

Donor: Undergoes medical and psychological (for living donors) evaluation.

Recipient: Patients medical history and health condition are evaluated to deem the patient suitable for transplant.

Step 3
Placement

Donor: Once approved, the donor is scheduled for transplant surgery.

Recipient: If deemed eligible, the patient is placed on a waiting list based on medical urgency and compatibility.

Step 4
Matching

Donor: Tests are done to determine if the donor's organ is a match for the recipient.

Recipient: Tests are done to determine if the recipient's body will accept the donated organ.

Step 5
Surgery

Donor: Undergoes surgery to remove the donated organ(s).

Recipient: Undergoes a surgical procedure to receive the organ.

Step 6
Postoperative

Donor: Living Donor is monitored and receives postoperative care, receives follow-up care to ensure recovery.

Recipient: Monitored for signs of organ rejection and complications, receives follow-up care to monitor adjustments.
